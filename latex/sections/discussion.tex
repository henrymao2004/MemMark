\section{Discussion}

\paragraph{Why state-evolution, not action.}
\todo{Summarize the trajectory-vs-state argument from \S\ref{sec:intro}
plus the empirical R3 vs Action-layer comparison from
\S\ref{sec:rq3}. The single line: action-layer attribution rates
collapse to zero exactly when attribution matters most (memory has
left the original system, trajectory has been pruned).}

\paragraph{What the distribution-preservation guarantee buys, and what it doesn't.}
\todo{Lemma \ref{lem:dist-preserve} is a per-decision marginal
guarantee. We do \emph{not} claim end-to-end answer-distribution
preservation across the full QA pipeline — the $\sim$7\% relative
F1 drop in \S\ref{sec:rq1} is the empirical price of biasing
selection toward the keyed pick. \texttt{signed-metadata-only}
matches \texttt{no-watermark} F1, confirming the cost is the
keyed-pick selection itself, not the audit-trace machinery around
it. Retrieval-aware sampling that preserves keyword distribution
is an obvious follow-up to narrow this gap.}

\paragraph{The trade-off shape.}
\todo{Table~\ref{tab:tradeoff} is the headline summary: metadata
provenance is free but unverifiable in adversarial storage;
trajectory-bound watermarks are unverifiable once the trajectory
is gone; \memmark{} is the only point that costs anything \emph{and}
delivers snapshot-only attribution. We argue the trade-off is the
right one for the forensic setting where the alternatives don't
function at all.}

\paragraph{Limitations.}
\todo{(i) Backend coverage limited to \amem{} / \graphiti{};
extending to MemOS / MemMachine is engineering. (ii) Adversary that
can both write the snapshot \emph{and} read the secret key trivially
forges; we assume the key is not exfiltrated. (iii) cat-5
adversarial questions remain hard; F1 lifted to $\approx 0.4$ after
the loader fix but still below paper-level for some backends. (iv)
\texttt{contradiction\_rate} / merge / delete / stale-memory /
temporal consistency need ground-truth labels and are not reported
in this draft.}

\paragraph{Future work.}
\todo{(i) Retrieval-aware sampler to close the RQ1 gap.
(ii) Larger $K$ — current self-reported enumeration is $K=4$;
$K{=}8$ doubles per-decision capacity but cost / quality trade-off
needs study. (iii) Online attestation for streaming agents.
(iv) Cross-session anchor chaining for long-running deployments.}
